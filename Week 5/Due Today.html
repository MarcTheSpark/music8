Due Week 5

  • Sound Recording Collection: Collect 8-12 recordings of sounds that you find interesting, either by recording them with your phone or portable recording device, or by downloading them from an online service such as freesound.org. Try to have each recording contain just one single "sound object", editing the files where necessary using a tool such as Audacity. Name each recording something simple that describes its contents, such as "alarmclock.wav". Place these sounds in a single folder with your name on it, compress the folder, and e-mail it to me as a downloadable link (e.g. from google drive, box, dropbox, wetransfer) by Sunday at 5pm.

  • If you're using an android phone, a good option for making a recording and emailing it to yourself is Audio Recorder. (Use this link if possible, because there are many similarly named programs, and I think this is the best one.)

  • Sound Categorization: Using the template in "Week 5/Listening Graph Templates/Scales.pdf", place each sound where you feel it belongs on the various scales given. For the last two, invent some properties of your own, such as "sudden attack vs. gradual attack" or "stable vs. unstable". Having done this, use the template in "Week 5/Listening Graph Templates/2D Graphs.pdf" to compare how sounds relate based on two different properties. (You can reuse the same properties as before in the 1D scales.) Bring these filled-out templates to class.

  • Keep in Mind: Just as before, your next assignment will be to turn these sounds into a composition, so make them interesting!
