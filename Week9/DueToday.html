  • Final Project Proposal: The final project for Music 8 will be to write a somewhat more substantial composition. It can be an extension of one of the assignments, or it can be something totally different. The key requirements / goals for this project are:
    • It should be something that you consider to be music, and that you can convincingly argue is music. If you're inclined to do something more "out there" (which I would welcome!), take a look at some of these links for inspiration:
      • I Am Sitting In A Room (for which you should definitely check out the Wikipedia Article to understand what's happening)
      • The Metaphysics of Notation
      • The Ursonata: score and performance. Is this music? Poetry? Both? Neither?
      • The difference between hearing and listening
    • We should be able to hear it somehow. If you're writing for instruments, I strongly encourage you to either write for an instrument you play or work with an performer.
    • There should be a "score" of some form. This could be traditional western music notation, or it could be something more adventurous, like a graphic score, or a series of instructions for how to produce the sound. If you're creating a piece of electronic music, you should be able to present, along with the music, a visual representation of the structure and material of the piece. E.g. refer to Ligeti's Artikulation.
    • Keep it manageable: it will essentially be a two-week project, so propose something that excites you, but that is realistic to finish in that time-frame.
    By 5pm on Sunday Nov. 18, I would like you to e-mail to me (attached as a PDF) a roughly 300-word proposal for your final project that addresses:
    • The inspirations for your project, preferably with one or two links
    • The sound materials you will use (or the instrument or ensemble you will write for)
    • The tools you will use to create the music. (Pencil and paper? A notation program? Audacity?)
    • How long will the piece be?
    • How are we going to hear the music? (e.g. who's performing it)
    • How are we going to see the music? (what will the score look like)
    • What do you need from me to help you complete the project?
