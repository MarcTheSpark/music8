  • Reading Discussion:
    • Group 1: "Sitting in a parking lot on my third day of article writing, I could listen to the sterophony of car starter gagglings, motor wigglings, door squeals, and "bllaps" forever. It's almost like Debussy, compared to Saturday's Wagnerian bulldozer"
      • What does she mean by "stereophony" here? Read some of this
      • Why are the sounds like Debussy, why was the bulldozer Wagnerian? Take a listen here and here, and to other works of Debussy and Wagner.
    • Group 2: "A faraway jet simulates a fifty foot tabla, accompanied by an infinite freeway tamboura"
      • What does she mean by this comparison?
      • See the wikipedia pages on Tabla and Tanpura, as well as this sample of them playing together.
      • Here's an actual performance. The Tanpura and Tabla players are the two furthest on the right.
    • Group 3: "During the quiet evening of a summer vacation near the Feather River Canyon, Lynn, Bob, and I wanted to play music. We decided to read John Cage's Atlas Eclipticalis from the original score, which was still shining brightly above".
      • What is John Cage's Atlas Eclipticalis? What does she mean by playing it from the original score?
      • Read up on it here.
      • Take a look at this webpage. It's fun.
    • Group 4: "As I lean on my wooden table, my arm receives sympathetic vibrations from the low frequencies of the bulldozer" ... "7Hz at high intensity can make you sick or kill you"
      • What are "sympathetic vibrations"? See this wikipedia page.
      • With this video of the Tacoma Narrows Bridge Collapse in mind, how might 7Hz at high intensity make you sick?
    • Extra challenge, what's going on with this quote: "walking toward the theater, my footsteps greeted me from the approaching wall; midway, they followed me from the opposite wall".
      • Why would they approach from in front, and then come from behind?
