  • Reading: "Shitty First Drafts" from Bird by Bird by Anne Lamott. This is especially there for you if you're struggling with the composition exercise. You do not need to send me a reading response for this reading (though I guess you're welcome to if you want...).

  • Composition Assignment 1: Your first composition assignment will be to take two of the sounds that you found in last week's assignment and to develop them into a piece of music. The score is to take the form of a graph of the main expressive parameter for each instrument. Here are a couple of examples to give you a better sense of the assignment:

    • "Popsicle / Ruler": Score, Description, Recording
    • "Clicks and Whispers": Score, Description, Recording. Notice that here, the timing has been quantized to a grid, which is one option you have available.

    I've provided two manuscript templates for you, one of which suggests fluid timing, and the other of which suggests quantized timing. In addition, if one of the instruments you choose has a discrete range of variation, you can use horizontal lines to distiguish different sounds. For instance, a score for a cup that you hit in different places might look like this.

    A few other guidelines:
    • Along with the composition itself, turn in a short description of what your two instruments are and what parameter of sound you're manipulating for each instrument.
    • Give some indication on the score of how fast the music is supposed to go. For the unquantized version of the score, a good approach is to give an approximate number of seconds per system. For the quantized score, you can give a metronome marking for the beat.
    • Aim for the piece to be about 2 or 3 pages, but effort is more important than length.
    • If you want, you can make the graph of the parameter get thicker or thinner to express dynamics. I did this in one of my examples.
